## Script 1: Body Scan Meditation
**Duration:** 10 minutes  
**Purpose:** Deep body awareness, tension release, grounding

### Audio Pacing Notes
- Speak slowly and deliberately
- Pause 3-5 seconds between body regions
- Use a calm, warm tone throughout
- Allow 10-15 seconds of silence at the end

### Background Sound Recommendation
- Soft ambient drone at 432Hz
- Gentle nature sounds (light rain or forest ambience)
- Volume: 20-30% of voice level

### Full Script

**[Opening - 0:00-1:00]**

Welcome to this body scan meditation. Find a comfortable position, either lying down on your back or sitting in a chair with your feet flat on the floor. Allow your arms to rest gently at your sides, palms facing upward if that feels comfortable.

Take a moment to settle in. Let your eyes close gently, or soften your gaze if you prefer to keep them open. There is nothing you need to do right now, nowhere you need to go. This time is yours.

**[Initial Grounding - 1:00-2:00]**

Begin by taking three deep, cleansing breaths. Inhale deeply through your nose... feeling your lungs expand... and exhale slowly through your mouth... releasing any tension you may be holding.

Again... inhale deeply... and exhale completely.

One more time... breathe in peace and calm... and breathe out any stress or worry.

**[Body Scan - 2:00-8:30]**

Now, bring your attention to the very top of your head. Notice any sensations there—perhaps a gentle tingling, warmth, or simply the feeling of your hair against your scalp. There's no need to change anything. Just observe.

Slowly, allow your awareness to drift down to your forehead. Notice if there is any tension there. If you feel tightness, imagine it melting away like warm wax, softening and releasing.

Move your attention to your eyes. Let them be heavy and relaxed. Release any tension around your eye sockets, your temples, your jaw. Allow your jaw to hang slightly open if that feels comfortable.

Now feel your neck and throat. Let the muscles here be soft and at ease. Imagine any stress dissolving with each exhale.

Bring your awareness to your shoulders. These often hold so much of our daily stress. On your next exhale, consciously let your shoulders drop away from your ears, releasing any burden they've been carrying.

Move down to your upper arms... your elbows... your forearms. Feel the weight of your arms, heavy and relaxed.

Now your hands. Notice your palms, your fingers, the spaces between your fingers. Feel any warmth or tingling here. Your hands are completely at rest.

Shift your attention to your chest. Feel the gentle rise and fall with each breath. Your heart is beating steadily, calmly. There is peace here.

Move to your upper back. Notice any sensations along your spine, your shoulder blades. Let this area soften and release.

Now your abdomen. Feel it expand with each inhale and gently contract with each exhale. There is no need to control your breathing—just observe its natural rhythm.

Bring awareness to your lower back. If there is any tension, imagine it dissolving, melting away into the surface beneath you.

Move to your hips and pelvis. Let them be heavy, sinking comfortably into your support.

Now your thighs. Feel the muscles here releasing any tension. Your legs are becoming heavier and more relaxed with each breath.

Your knees... your calves... feel any sensations in these areas without judgment.

Finally, bring your attention to your feet. Your heels, the arches, the balls of your feet, your toes. Feel the connection between your feet and the ground, or the surface beneath you. You are supported. You are grounded.

**[Full Body Awareness - 8:30-9:30]**

Now, take a moment to sense your entire body as a whole. From the top of your head to the tips of your toes, you are present, aware, and at peace. Feel the energy flowing through you, connecting every part of your being.

**[Closing - 9:30-10:00]**

When you're ready, begin to bring your awareness back to the room around you. Wiggle your fingers and toes gently. Take a deep breath in... and release it slowly.

When you feel ready, gently open your eyes. Carry this sense of calm and body awareness with you into the rest of your day.

Namaste.

## Script 2: Loving-Kindness Meditation (Metta)
**Duration:** 10 minutes  
**Purpose:** Cultivate compassion, love, and connection

### Audio Pacing Notes
- Speak with genuine warmth and sincerity
- Pause 5-7 seconds after each phrase to let it sink in
- Gradually increase emotional intensity
- End with a sense of expansive love

### Background Sound Recommendation
- Soft choral pads or gentle singing bowls
- Heart chakra frequency (341.3Hz) subtly mixed
- Volume: 25% of voice level

### Full Script

**[Opening - 0:00-1:00]**

Welcome to this loving-kindness meditation. Find a comfortable seated position, keeping your spine straight but not rigid. Rest your hands on your heart or in your lap, whichever feels most natural.

Close your eyes gently and take a few deep breaths. With each inhale, feel your heart space expanding. With each exhale, release any barriers around your heart.

**[Self-Directed Loving-Kindness - 1:00-3:30]**

We begin by directing loving-kindness toward ourselves. This is not selfish—it is essential. We cannot pour from an empty cup.

Bring to mind an image of yourself. See yourself happy, healthy, and at peace.

Now, silently repeat these phrases, feeling their meaning in your heart:

May I be happy.

May I be healthy.

May I be safe.

May I live with ease.

Repeat these phrases again, really feeling each word:

May I be happy.

May I be healthy.

May I be safe.

May I live with ease.

One more time, with deep sincerity:

May I be happy.

May I be healthy.

May I be safe.

May I live with ease.

**[Loved One - 3:30-5:30]**

Now, bring to mind someone you love deeply. This could be a family member, a close friend, a partner, or even a beloved pet. Visualize them clearly, see their face, feel your love for them.

Direct these phrases toward them:

May you be happy.

May you be healthy.

May you be safe.

May you live with ease.

Again, with all your heart:

May you be happy.

May you be healthy.

May you be safe.

May you live with ease.

**[Neutral Person - 5:30-7:00]**

Now, think of someone neutral—someone you see regularly but don't have strong feelings about. Perhaps a cashier at a store, a neighbor you pass, or a colleague you don't know well.

Hold them in your awareness and offer them these wishes:

May you be happy.

May you be healthy.

May you be safe.

May you live with ease.

**[Difficult Person - 7:00-8:30]**

This next part can be challenging, but it is transformative. Bring to mind someone with whom you have difficulty. It doesn't need to be someone who has caused you great harm—just someone with whom there is tension or conflict.

See if you can hold them in your awareness without judgment. Remember that, like you, they too want to be happy. They too suffer. Offer them these phrases:

May you be happy.

May you be healthy.

May you be safe.

May you live with ease.

**[All Beings - 8:30-9:30]**

Now, expand your loving-kindness to include all beings everywhere. Imagine your love radiating outward like ripples in a pond, touching everyone and everything.

All beings everywhere...

May all beings be happy.

May all beings be healthy.

May all beings be safe.

May all beings live with ease.

**[Closing - 9:30-10:00]**

Take a deep breath in, gathering all this loving energy into your heart. As you exhale, release it outward to the world.

When you're ready, gently open your eyes. Carry this loving-kindness with you, sharing it through your words, actions, and presence throughout your day.

Namaste.

## Script 3: Breath Awareness Meditation
**Duration:** 5 minutes  
**Purpose:** Quick centering, stress relief, present-moment awareness

### Audio Pacing Notes
- Keep pace brisk but calm
- Minimal pauses (2-3 seconds)
- Focus on guiding attention to breath
- End with gentle return to activity

### Background Sound Recommendation
- Soft white noise or gentle wind
- Very subtle, non-distracting
- Volume: 15-20% of voice level

### Full Script

**[Opening - 0:00-0:30]**

Welcome to this brief breath awareness meditation. Find a comfortable seated position. You can do this anywhere—at your desk, on a bus, or in a quiet room.

Close your eyes if that feels comfortable, or soften your gaze downward.

**[Breath Focus - 0:30-4:00]**

Bring your full attention to your breath. You don't need to change it in any way—just observe it as it naturally flows in and out.

Notice the cool air entering your nostrils on the inhale...

And the warm air leaving on the exhale...

Follow each breath from its beginning... through its middle... to its end.

If your mind wanders—and it will—that's completely normal. Simply notice the thought, let it go, and gently return your attention to your breath.

Your breath is an anchor, always available to bring you back to the present moment.

Breathe in... and breathe out.

In... and out.

There's nowhere else you need to be right now. Just here, with your breath.

**[Deepening - 4:00-4:30]**

Take three deeper breaths with me.

Breathe in deeply... and release.

Again... inhale fully... and let it go.

One more time... breathe in calm... and breathe out any remaining tension.

**[Closing - 4:30-5:00]**

You can return to this practice anytime you need a moment of calm. Your breath is always with you, a constant source of peace and presence.

When you're ready, gently open your eyes and return to your day, carrying this sense of calm with you.

## Script 4: Progressive Muscle Relaxation
**Duration:** 15 minutes  
**Purpose:** Deep physical relaxation, stress relief, better sleep preparation

### Audio Pacing Notes
- Clear, instructive tone for tensing
- Softer, releasing tone for relaxation
- 5-second hold for each tension phase
- 10-15 seconds for relaxation phase

### Background Sound Recommendation
- Gentle ocean waves or soft rain
- Steady, rhythmic background
- Volume: 25% of voice level

### Full Script

**[Opening - 0:00-1:30]**

Welcome to this progressive muscle relaxation meditation. This practice involves systematically tensing and then releasing different muscle groups in your body. This helps you become more aware of physical tension and teaches your body how to deeply relax.

Find a comfortable position lying down on your back. Allow your arms to rest at your sides, palms facing up. Let your legs fall naturally apart, feet falling outward.

Close your eyes and take a few deep breaths, settling into this space.

**[Hands and Forearms - 1:30-3:00]**

We'll begin with your hands and forearms.

Make tight fists with both hands. Feel the tension building in your fingers, your palms, your forearms. Hold this tension... five... four... three... two... one...

Now release. Let your hands open naturally, palms facing up. Feel the warmth and relaxation flowing into your hands and forearms. Notice the difference between tension and relaxation.

**[Upper Arms - 3:00-4:00]**

Now bring your attention to your upper arms.

Tense your biceps by drawing your forearms toward your shoulders, making your muscles tight. Hold this tension... five... four... three... two... one...

Release. Let your arms fall heavy onto the surface beneath you. Feel the relaxation spreading through your entire arms.

**[Face and Jaw - 4:00-5:30]**

Move to your face. Scrunch up all your facial muscles—furrow your brow, squeeze your eyes shut, wrinkle your nose, and tighten your jaw. Hold... five... four... three... two... one...

Release completely. Let your face be smooth and soft. Allow your jaw to hang slightly open. Feel the relaxation in your forehead, your eyes, your cheeks, your jaw.

**[Neck and Shoulders - 5:30-7:00]**

Now your neck and shoulders.

Draw your shoulders up toward your ears, making them as tight as you can. Feel the tension in your neck and shoulder muscles. Hold... five... four... three... two... one...

Release. Let your shoulders drop away from your ears, heavy and relaxed. Feel any stress melting away from this area where we so often hold tension.

**[Chest and Back - 7:00-8:30]**

Bring awareness to your chest and upper back.

Take a deep breath in and hold it, tightening your chest and back muscles. Hold... five... four... three... two... one...

Exhale completely and release. Let your chest be soft, your back relaxed against your support.

**[Abdomen - 8:30-9:30]**

Now your abdomen.

Tighten your stomach muscles, pulling your navel toward your spine. Make your abdomen as firm as you can. Hold... five... four... three... two... one...

Release. Let your belly be soft and relaxed. Allow it to rise and fall naturally with your breath.

**[Hips and Buttocks - 9:30-10:30]**

Move to your hips and buttocks.

Tighten these muscles, squeezing your glutes together. Hold the tension... five... four... three... two... one...

Release. Let your hips be heavy, sinking comfortably into your support.

**[Thighs - 10:30-11:30]**

Now your thighs.

Tighten the large muscles in your thighs, pressing your legs into the surface beneath you. Hold... five... four... three... two... one...

Release. Feel your thighs becoming heavy and relaxed.

**[Calves - 11:30-12:30]**

Bring attention to your calves.

Point your toes toward your face, tensing your calf muscles. Hold... five... four... three... two... one...

Release. Let your calves be soft and at ease.

**[Feet - 12:30-13:30]**

Finally, your feet.

Curl your toes tightly, tensing all the muscles in your feet. Hold... five... four... three... two... one...

Release. Let your feet fall outward naturally, completely relaxed.

**[Full Body Relaxation - 13:30-14:30]**

Now, scan your entire body. Notice how much more relaxed you feel compared to when you began. Every muscle group has released its tension. You are deeply relaxed.

If you notice any remaining areas of tension, imagine breathing relaxation into those areas with each inhale, and releasing tension with each exhale.

**[Closing - 14:30-15:00]**

When you're ready to return, begin by wiggling your fingers and toes. Take a deep breath in, stretching if that feels good. Gently open your eyes.

Carry this deep relaxation with you. You can return to this practice anytime you need to release physical tension and stress.

## Script 5: Visualization - Safe Place
**Duration:** 10 minutes  
**Purpose:** Create inner sanctuary, emotional safety, stress relief

### Audio Pacing Notes
- Use descriptive, evocative language
- Pause 5-7 seconds between descriptions
- Speak softly, inviting imagination
- Allow time for the scene to develop

### Background Sound Recommendation
- Gentle ambient music with nature elements
- Birdsong or gentle stream if appropriate
- Volume: 30% of voice level

### Full Script

**[Opening - 0:00-1:00]**

Welcome to this safe place visualization. This meditation will guide you to create or visit a place within your imagination where you feel completely safe, peaceful, and at ease.

Find a comfortable position, either sitting or lying down. Close your eyes and take a few deep breaths, allowing yourself to settle into this moment.

**[Journey to Safe Place - 1:00-2:30]**

Imagine yourself walking along a gentle path. This path leads to your safe place—a location that exists just for you, where you feel completely protected and at peace.

As you walk, notice the ground beneath your feet. Is it soft grass? Smooth stones? Warm sand? Feel each step grounding you, bringing you closer to your sanctuary.

The air around you is perfect—neither too warm nor too cool. It feels gentle against your skin, inviting and comforting.

**[Arriving - 2:30-4:30]**

You arrive at your safe place. Take a moment to look around and see what is here.

Perhaps it's a cozy cabin in the woods, with a warm fire crackling inside. Maybe it's a sunny beach with gentle waves lapping at the shore. It could be a peaceful garden filled with flowers, or a mountaintop with endless views. It might be a room from your childhood, or a place you've only ever imagined.

This is your place. It can be anything you want it to be.

Notice the colors around you. Are they bright and vibrant, or soft and muted? Let your eyes take in every detail.

**[Engaging the Senses - 4:30-7:00]**

Now, engage all your senses in this place.

What do you hear? Perhaps birds singing, water flowing, wind rustling through leaves, or blessed silence. Let these sounds wash over you, adding to your sense of peace.

What do you smell? Fresh air, flowers, the scent of rain, or something that brings you comfort. Breathe in these scents deeply.

What can you touch? Run your hand over a surface nearby. Feel its texture—smooth, rough, soft, cool, warm. This place is real to your senses.

If there is something to taste here—a fresh fruit, cool water, warm tea—imagine its flavor, satisfying and nourishing.

**[Deepening the Experience - 7:00-8:30]**

Find a place to sit or lie down in your safe space. Make yourself completely comfortable.

As you rest here, feel a sense of deep peace washing over you. In this place, you are completely safe. Nothing can harm you here. No worries can reach you. This is your sanctuary, available to you anytime you need it.

Feel your body relaxing more deeply with each breath. The safety of this place seeps into every cell of your being.

**[Affirmation - 8:30-9:15]**

Silently repeat to yourself:

I am safe.

I am at peace.

This place is always within me.

I can return here whenever I need.

**[Closing - 9:15-10:00]**

Know that this safe place exists within you always. You have created it, and it is yours to return to whenever you need comfort, peace, or refuge.

When you're ready, begin to bring your awareness back to the room around you. Take a deep breath in... and exhale slowly.

Wiggle your fingers and toes. Gently open your eyes, carrying the peace of your safe place with you.

## Script 6: Visualization - Mountain Meditation
**Duration:** 10 minutes  
**Purpose:** Build resilience, stability, groundedness

### Audio Pacing Notes
- Strong, grounding tone
- Use imagery of solidity and permanence
- Pause to let the mountain image form
- End with sense of inner strength

### Background Sound Recommendation
- Deep, resonant drone or didgeridoo tones
- Subtle wind sounds
- Volume: 25% of voice level

### Full Script

**[Opening - 0:00-1:00]**

Welcome to the mountain meditation. This practice uses the image of a mountain to help you connect with your own inner stability, strength, and resilience.

Find a comfortable seated position, keeping your spine tall and strong like a mountain peak. Rest your hands on your thighs or in your lap.

Close your eyes and take a few deep breaths, grounding yourself in this moment.

**[The Mountain Form - 1:00-3:00]**

Imagine a majestic mountain before you. It rises tall and strong from the earth, its base wide and grounded, its peak reaching toward the sky.

This mountain has stood for thousands of years. It has witnessed countless seasons, countless changes. It remains, unshakable, through it all.

Now, imagine that you are this mountain. Feel yourself becoming solid, grounded, and immovable. Your base extends deep into the earth, connecting you to something ancient and stable.

**[The Mountain's Qualities - 3:00-5:30]**

As the mountain, you are present for all that occurs around you.

In the morning, the sun rises and warms your slopes. You receive its light without clinging to it.

In the evening, the sun sets and darkness falls. You release the light without resisting the dark.

Storms come—fierce winds, driving rain, lightning splitting the sky. You remain anchored, letting the storm rage around you. You do not fight it. You do not fear it. You simply are.

Seasons change. Spring brings new life to your slopes. Summer warms your stones. Autumn paints you in brilliant colors. Winter covers you in snow and ice. Through all of this, you remain. You do not prefer one season over another. You witness each with equanimity.

**[Your Inner Mountain - 5:30-8:00]**

Just as this mountain remains steady through all external changes, you too have this capacity for inner stability.

The thoughts that pass through your mind are like clouds passing over the mountain peak. They come, they go. They do not change what you are.

The emotions that arise are like weather patterns—sometimes pleasant, sometimes difficult. They move through you without defining you.

The events of your life are like the seasons—constantly changing, never permanent. You can witness them all with the same grounded presence as the mountain.

Feel this stability within you now. It has always been there, waiting to be recognized.

**[Affirmation - 8:00-9:00]**

Silently repeat these words, feeling their truth:

I am grounded.

I am stable.

I am resilient.

I remain, through all that comes and goes.

**[Closing - 9:00-10:00]**

Carry the image of the mountain with you. When life feels chaotic or uncertain, remember this place of stability within yourself. You are stronger than you know. You can weather any storm.

Take a deep breath in, gathering this strength. Exhale slowly, releasing any tension.

When you're ready, gently open your eyes, grounded and centered, ready to meet whatever comes.

## Script 7: Sleep Meditation
**Duration:** 20 minutes  
**Purpose:** Deep relaxation, prepare for restful sleep

### Audio Pacing Notes
- Very slow, soothing pace
- Long pauses (8-10 seconds)
- Soft, drowsy tone
- Gradually fade toward silence

### Background Sound Recommendation
- Deep delta wave frequencies (0.5-4Hz)
- Gentle rain or soft ocean waves
- Volume: 20% of voice level, gradually decreasing

### Full Script

**[Opening - 0:00-2:00]**

Welcome to this sleep meditation. This practice is designed to help you release the day and drift into deep, restful sleep.

Lie down in your bed, in your most comfortable sleeping position. Allow your body to be fully supported by your mattress, your pillow, your blankets.

Close your eyes gently. There's nothing you need to do right now. Nowhere you need to go. Simply allow yourself to be here, in this moment, preparing for sleep.

**[Releasing the Day - 2:00-5:00]**

Take a moment to acknowledge the day that has passed. Whatever happened—meetings, conversations, tasks completed or left undone—let it all go now. The day is over. You have done enough. You are enough.

With each exhale, imagine releasing the events of the day. Let them float away like leaves on a stream, drifting further and further from you.

Your work is done. Your responsibilities can wait until tomorrow. Right now, your only job is to rest.

**[Body Relaxation - 5:00-10:00]**

Bring your attention to your toes. Feel them relaxing completely, growing heavy and warm. This relaxation spreads through your feet... your ankles... your calves.

Your legs are becoming heavier and heavier, sinking comfortably into your bed. There is no need to hold any tension. Just let go.

Feel your hips and pelvis releasing, softening, melting into your mattress.

Your abdomen is relaxed, rising and falling gently with each soft breath.

Your chest is open and at ease. Your heart beats slowly, steadily, calmly.

Your back releases all tension, from your lower back up through your shoulders.

Your arms are heavy, completely relaxed, resting at your sides.

Your hands are soft, fingers slightly curled, completely at rest.

Your neck is long and relaxed. Your jaw hangs slightly open. Your face is smooth and peaceful.

Your forehead is soft, free from any furrows or tension.

Your entire scalp is relaxed.

Your whole body is now deeply relaxed, heavy, and ready for sleep.

**[Counting Down - 10:00-14:00]**

Now, we'll count down from ten to one. With each number, allow yourself to sink deeper into relaxation, closer to sleep.

Ten... feeling your body heavy and relaxed.

Nine... sinking deeper into your bed.

Eight... your mind becoming quiet and still.

Seven... each breath bringing you closer to sleep.

Six... your thoughts slowing down, becoming distant.

Five... half-way to deep, restful sleep.

Four... your body is completely at rest.

Three... sinking deeper and deeper.

Two... almost there, so relaxed, so peaceful.

One... drifting now into deep, restorative sleep.

**[Sleep Invitation - 14:00-18:00]**

You are now in the perfect state for sleep. Your body is relaxed. Your mind is calm. There is nothing to do but let go completely.

If thoughts arise, simply acknowledge them and let them pass. Return to the feeling of your breath, the heaviness of your body, the comfort of your bed.

You are safe. You are comfortable. Sleep will come naturally.

Allow yourself to drift now. Let go of any effort. Simply be.

**[Silent Transition - 18:00-20:00]**

[Continue with soft background sounds only. If voice continues, use only simple, repetitive phrases:]

Drifting... resting... sleeping...

Deep, peaceful sleep...

All is well...

Rest now...

[Fade to silence or continue with background sounds only]

## Script 9: Anxiety Relief Meditation
**Duration:** 7 minutes  
**Purpose:** Calm anxious thoughts, regulate nervous system

### Audio Pacing Notes
- Calm, reassuring tone throughout
- Slower pace to counter anxiety
- Pause after grounding techniques
- Emphasize safety and control

### Background Sound Recommendation
- 528Hz healing frequency (love and miracles)
- Soft heartbeat rhythm or gentle rain
- Volume: 25% of voice level

### Full Script

**[Opening - 0:00-1:00]**

Welcome. If you're feeling anxious right now, know that you're not alone, and this feeling will pass. This meditation is here to help you find calm and relief.

Find a comfortable seated position. If possible, place your feet flat on the floor. This helps ground you in the present moment.

You are safe. You are in control. Let's take this journey together, one breath at a time.

**[Grounding - 1:00-2:30]**

Let's begin with a simple grounding technique. I want you to notice five things you can see right now. Look around and name them silently to yourself.

Now, notice four things you can touch. Feel the texture of your clothing, the surface beneath you, the air on your skin.

Notice three things you can hear. Listen for sounds in the room, outside, or the sound of your own breath.

Notice two things you can smell. Take a gentle breath through your nose.

Notice one thing you can taste.

You are here. You are present. You are safe.

**[Breathing Technique - 2:30-4:30]**

Now, let's use a breathing technique to calm your nervous system.

Place one hand on your chest and one hand on your belly.

Breathe in slowly through your nose for a count of four... one... two... three... four.

Hold your breath gently for a count of four... one... two... three... four.

Exhale slowly through your mouth for a count of six... one... two... three... four... five... six.

Let's do this together two more times.

Inhale... two... three... four.

Hold... two... three... four.

Exhale... two... three... four... five... six.

One more time.

Inhale... two... three... four.

Hold... two... three... four.

Exhale... two... three... four... five... six.

**[Releasing Thoughts - 4:30-6:00]**

Anxiety often comes from thoughts about the future—worries about what might happen. Let's practice letting these thoughts go.

Imagine your anxious thoughts as clouds floating across the sky. You are the sky—vast, open, calm. The thoughts are just clouds passing through. They don't define you. They don't control you. They simply pass.

With each exhale, imagine releasing one anxious thought, watching it float away on the cloud.

You don't need to solve anything right now. You don't need to figure anything out. You only need to be here, breathing, present.

**[Affirmation - 6:00-6:45]**

Repeat these affirmations silently:

I am safe in this moment.

This feeling will pass.

I can handle whatever comes.

I am stronger than my anxiety.

**[Closing - 6:45-7:00]**

You have tools now. The grounding technique. The breathing exercise. The cloud visualization. Use them whenever anxiety arises.

Remember: anxiety is a feeling, not a fact. It will pass, as all feelings do.

Take one more deep breath. When you're ready, gently open your eyes. You've got this.

## Script 10: Grief Processing Meditation
**Duration:** 12 minutes  
**Purpose:** Honor loss, process emotions, find gentle healing

### Audio Pacing Notes
- Gentle, compassionate tone
- Slower pace with longer pauses
- Allow space for emotions to arise
