## Anxiety

*30 prompts to help process anxious thoughts and find calm*

1. What is one worry that keeps recurring for you? Write about where it might come from.

2. Describe a time when you felt anxious but things turned out okay. What helped you through?

3. What does anxiety feel like in your body? Describe the physical sensations without judgment.

4. Write a letter to your anxiety. What would you want it to know?

5. List five things you can see, four you can touch, three you can hear, two you can smell, and one you can taste right now.

6. What triggers your anxiety most often? Can you identify any patterns?

7. If your anxiety had a voice, what would it say? Now, write a compassionate response.

8. What is the worst-case scenario you're afraid of? What is a more realistic outcome?

9. Describe your "safe place" in detail. What makes it feel secure?

10. What coping strategies have helped you manage anxiety in the past?

11. Write about a time you faced a fear. How did you feel afterward?

12. What would you tell a friend who was experiencing the same anxiety you are?

13. List three things that are within your control right now.

14. What does "calm" look like for you? Describe it vividly.

15. What negative thought patterns do you notice when you're anxious?

16. Write about something you're anxious about, then list three possible positive outcomes.

17. What self-care activities help ground you when anxiety rises?

18. Describe your anxiety as if it were a weather pattern. Is it a storm, fog, or something else?

19. What would your life look like without this particular worry?

20. Write about a time you surprised yourself with your resilience.

21. What small step can you take today to feel more at ease?

22. How does your anxiety try to protect you? Can you thank it and let it go?

23. What affirmations help you when anxiety feels overwhelming?

24. Describe your breath. Is it shallow, fast, slow? How does it change as you write?

25. What is one thing you can release control of today?

26. Write about a place in nature that makes you feel peaceful.

27. What would you do today if you weren't afraid?

28. List ten things that make you feel safe and grounded.

29. How has your relationship with anxiety changed over time?

30. What is one kind thing you can say to yourself when anxiety appears?

## How to Use These Prompts

### Getting Started
- Choose a prompt that resonates with how you're feeling today
- Set aside 10-20 minutes of uninterrupted time
- Write without editing or judging your thoughts
- There's no right or wrong way to respond

### Creating a Practice
- Try to journal at the same time each day
- Start with just one prompt per session
- Return to prompts that feel particularly meaningful
- Notice patterns in your responses over time

### When You're Stuck
- Write about why the prompt feels difficult
- Choose a different prompt that feels easier
- Simply write "I don't know what to say" until something emerges
- Remember that showing up is what matters most
