## 1. 4-7-8 Breathing

### Purpose
Activates the parasympathetic nervous system to rapidly reduce anxiety, slow heart rate, and promote sleep. Based on ancient pranayama techniques adapted by Dr. Andrew Weil.

### When to Use
- Before bed to fall asleep faster
- During anxiety or panic attacks
- Before stressful presentations or meetings
- When feeling overwhelmed
- To manage cravings

### Audio-Ready Guided Script (90 seconds)

**[Soft ambient music fades in]**

"Welcome to 4-7-8 breathing, your natural tranquilizer.

Find a comfortable position. You can do this sitting or lying down. Gently close your eyes or soften your gaze.

Let your shoulders drop away from your ears. Release any tension in your jaw.

Place the tip of your tongue gently against the ridge behind your upper front teeth. Keep it there throughout this practice.

Now, let all the air out through your mouth with a soft whoosh sound.

**Inhale** quietly through your nose for four... three... two... one...

**Hold** your breath for seven... six... five... four... three... two... one...

**Exhale** completely through your mouth with a whoosh for eight... seven... six... five... four... three... two... one...

Again. Empty your lungs completely.

**Inhale** for four... three... two... one...

**Hold** for seven... six... five... four... three... two... one...

**Exhale** with a whoosh for eight... seven... six... five... four... three... two... one...

One more cycle. Release all tension.

**Inhale** for four... three... two... one...

**Hold** for seven... six... five... four... three... two... one...

**Exhale** with a whoosh for eight... seven... six... five... four... three... two... one...

Beautiful. Notice how your body feels calmer, your mind quieter.

This technique becomes more powerful with practice. You can use it anytime, anywhere.

**[Music fades out]**"

### Visual Animation Cues
- **Central Element**: A softly glowing orb or lotus flower
- **Inhale (4s)**: Orb expands from center, color shifts from soft blue to warm gold
- **Hold (7s)**: Orb pulses gently with a steady golden glow, subtle particle effects
- **Exhale (8s)**: Orb contracts and releases flowing particles outward like dandelion seeds
- **Background**: Deep indigo gradient with subtle star-like particles
- **Text Overlay**: "Inhale 4... Hold 7... Exhale 8" with countdown numbers
- **Audio Sync**: Gentle bell tone at each phase transition

## 2. Box Breathing

### Purpose
Enhances focus, reduces stress, and improves performance under pressure. Used by Navy SEALs and high-performance athletes to maintain calm in high-stress situations.

### When to Use
- Before important meetings or presentations
- During high-pressure work situations
- To regain focus and mental clarity
- Before athletic performance
- When feeling scattered or distracted

### Audio-Ready Guided Script (85 seconds)

**[Clean, minimal tones begin]**

"Welcome to Box Breathing. This technique creates a perfect square of calm.

Sit comfortably with your spine tall. Rest your hands on your thighs or in your lap.

Close your eyes and bring your attention to your breath.

We'll breathe in a perfect box: four counts on each side.

Begin by exhaling completely.

**Inhale** for four... three... two... one...

**Hold** for four... three... two... one...

**Exhale** for four... three... two... one...

**Hold** empty for four... three... two... one...

Feel the rhythm. The square of your breath.

**Inhale**... four... three... two... one...

**Hold**... four... three... two... one...

**Exhale**... four... three... two... one...

**Hold**... four... three... two... one...

One more complete box.

**Inhale**... four... three... two... one...

**Hold**... four... three... two... one...

**Exhale**... four... three... two... one...

**Hold**... four... three... two... one...

Excellent. Feel the precision, the control, the calm.

Your mind is clear. Your body is steady. You're ready.

**[Tones fade out]**"

### Visual Animation Cues
- **Central Element**: A geometric square or diamond shape
- **Animation**: A glowing point travels along the perimeter of the square
- **Right side (Inhale 4s)**: Point moves upward, square fills with cool cyan color
- **Top side (Hold 4s)**: Point moves right, square maintains full cyan glow
- **Left side (Exhale 4s)**: Point moves downward, cyan drains from square

## 3. Diaphragmatic Breathing

### Purpose
Activates the diaphragm for deeper, more efficient breathing. Reduces stress hormones, lowers blood pressure, and promotes full-body relaxation.

### When to Use
- For general stress relief and relaxation
- To improve sleep quality
- When feeling physically tense
- To enhance meditation practice
- For better oxygenation during recovery

### Audio-Ready Guided Script (100 seconds)

**[Warm, flowing ambient music begins]**

"Welcome to Diaphragmatic Breathing. Let's breathe from your center.

Lie down or sit comfortably. If lying down, bend your knees slightly. If sitting, keep your spine tall.

Place one hand on your chest and one hand on your belly, just below your ribs.

The goal is to keep your chest hand still while your belly hand rises and falls.

Take a normal breath in through your nose... and out through your mouth.

Now, as you **inhale**, imagine your belly is a balloon filling with air. Feel your belly hand rise while your chest hand stays still.

**Inhale** slowly through your nose... two... three... four...

Feel your belly expand outward.

Now **exhale** slowly through pursed lips... two... three... four... five... six...

Feel your belly gently fall. Your chest remains calm.

Again. **Inhale** through your nose... belly rising... two... three... four...

**Exhale** through pursed lips... belly falling... two... three... four... five... six...

Let your shoulders relax. Let your jaw soften.

**Inhale**... belly expands... two... three... four...

**Exhale**... belly releases... two... three... four... five... six...

One more deep breath into your center.

**Inhale**... filling your balloon... two... three... four...

**Exhale**... slowly releasing... two... three... four... five... six...

Gently release your hands. Notice the calm throughout your body.

You've just breathed the way your body was designed to breathe.

**[Music fades out]**"

### Visual Animation Cues
- **Central Element**: A stylized human torso silhouette showing lungs and diaphragm
- **Inhale (4-6s)**: Diaphragm muscle descends, belly area expands with warm orange glow, lungs fill with soft blue
- **Exhale (6-8s)**: Diaphragm rises, belly contracts, lungs empty with gentle particle flow
- **Hand Indicators**: Two glowing hand icons show proper placement (chest and belly)
- **Background**: Soft gradient from warm peach to cool lavender
- **Text Overlay**: "Breathe into your belly" with rising/falling arrows
- **Audio Sync**: Deep, resonant tone on inhale; flowing water sound on exhale

## 4. Alternate Nostril Breathing (Nadi Shodhana)

### Purpose
Balances the left and right hemispheres of the brain, harmonizes the nervous system, and creates mental equilibrium. Purifies energy channels in the body.

### When to Use
- Before meditation for deeper practice
- When feeling mentally scattered or unbalanced
- To prepare for focused work or study
- During transitions between activities
- To regulate energy levels

### Audio-Ready Guided Script (110 seconds)

**[Soft, ethereal tones begin]**

"Welcome to Alternate Nostril Breathing. Find your inner balance.

Sit comfortably with your spine tall. Rest your left hand on your knee.

Bring your right hand to your face. Tuck your index and middle fingers. You'll use your thumb and ring finger.

Close your eyes and take a deep breath in... and out.

Close your right nostril with your thumb. **Inhale** through your left nostril... two... three... four...

Close your left nostril with your ring finger. Release your thumb. **Exhale** through your right nostril... two... three... four... five... six...

**Inhale** through your right nostril... two... three... four...

Close your right nostril. Release your left. **Exhale** through your left nostril... two... three... four... five... six...

This completes one round.

**Inhale** left... two... three... four...

Close left, **exhale** right... two... three... four... five... six...

**Inhale** right... two... three... four...

Close right, **exhale** left... two... three... four... five... six...

Feel the balance flowing through you.

**Inhale** left... two... three... four...

**Exhale** right... two... three... four... five... six...

**Inhale** right... two... three... four...

**Exhale** left... two... three... four... five... six...

Release your hand. Sit quietly for a moment.

Notice the harmony, the equilibrium within.

**[Tones fade out]**"

### Visual Animation Cues
- **Central Element**: A stylized face silhouette with two flowing energy channels (ida and pingala)
- **Left Nostril Phase**: Left channel glows with cool blue/moon energy, flowing upward
- **Right Nostril Phase**: Right channel glows with warm orange/sun energy, flowing upward
- **Central Channel**: Subtle purple glow at center (sushumna) that brightens with each complete round
- **Hand Position**: Animated hand showing proper mudra position
- **Background**: Split gradient - cool blue left, warm orange right, meeting in purple center
- **Text Overlay**: "Left" and "Right" indicators with flowing arrows
- **Audio Sync**: Soft chime alternates between left (lower tone) and right (higher tone)

## 6. 2:1 Breathing (Calm)

### Purpose
Extends the exhale to activate the parasympathetic nervous system more deeply. Creates profound relaxation and is excellent for preparing for sleep.

### When to Use
- Before sleep for deeper rest
- During high anxiety moments
- For deep relaxation
- After stressful events
- To quiet an overactive mind

### Audio-Ready Guided Script (85 seconds)

**[Soft, descending tones begin]**

"Welcome to 2:1 Breathing. Let your exhale carry away tension.

Lie down or sit comfortably. Close your eyes.

In this practice, your exhale is twice as long as your inhale. This deeply calms your nervous system.

**Inhale** through your nose for four counts... three... two... one...

**Exhale** through your mouth for eight counts... seven... six... five... four... three... two... one...

Feel the release.

**Inhale** for four... three... two... one...

**Exhale** for eight... seven... six... five... four... three... two... one...

Let each exhale take you deeper into calm.

**Inhale**... four... three... two... one...

**Exhale**... eight... seven... six... five... four... three... two... one...

Your body is settling. Your mind is quieting.

**Inhale**... four... three... two... one...

**Exhale**... eight... seven... six... five... four... three... two... one...

One more cycle. Let go completely.

**Inhale**... four... three... two... one...

**Exhale**... eight... seven... six... five... four... three... two... one...

Rest here. You've created space for peace.

**[Tones fade out]**"

### Visual Animation Cues
- **Central Element**: A flowing ribbon or stream
- **Inhale (4s)**: Ribbon flows upward in a gentle curve, color: soft teal
- **Exhale (8s)**: Ribbon flows downward in a longer, deeper curve, color: deep purple
- **Ratio Indicator**: Visual split showing 1/3 inhale, 2/3 exhale
- **Particle Effects**: Tension particles release and dissipate during exhale
- **Background**: Twilight gradient from deep purple to soft indigo

- **Audio Sync**: Rising tone on inhale, descending tone on exhale

## 7. Pursed Lip Breathing

### Purpose
Slows breathing rate, keeps airways open longer, and improves oxygen exchange. Particularly helpful for respiratory conditions and general breath control.

### When to Use
- During physical exertion
- For better breath control
- When feeling short of breath
- To manage COPD or asthma symptoms
- During recovery from illness

### Audio-Ready Guided Script (75 seconds)

**[Gentle, supportive tones begin]**

"Welcome to Pursed Lip Breathing. Find ease in every breath.

Sit comfortably with your shoulders relaxed.

This technique helps you breathe more efficiently by keeping your airways open.

**Inhale** slowly through your nose for two counts... one... two...

Now purse your lips as if you're about to whistle.

**Exhale** gently through pursed lips for four counts... three... two... one...

Feel the control.

**Inhale** through your nose... one... two...

Purse your lips.

**Exhale** through pursed lips... three... two... one...

Let your breath be slow and steady.

**Inhale**... one... two...

**Exhale**... three... two... one...

Your airways are open. Your breathing is calm.

**Inhale**... one... two...

**Exhale**... three... two... one...

One more time.

**Inhale**... one... two...

**Exhale**... three... two... one...

Well done. Your breath is your ally.

**[Tones fade out]**"

### Visual Animation Cues
- **Central Element**: A stylized pair of lips with airflow visualization
- **Inhale (2s)**: Air flows inward through nose, gentle blue stream
- **Pursed Position**: Lips briefly purse (0.5s transition)
- **Exhale (4s)**: Controlled stream flows outward, longer and steadier
- **Airway Visualization**: Throat/airway stays open and glowing throughout
- **Background**: Soft medical blue with subtle grid
- **Text Overlay**: "Nose in... Lips out" with lip position indicator
- **Audio Sync**: Soft whoosh on inhale, gentle whistle tone on exhale

Feel the energy, the release, the power within you.

**[Tones fade out]**"

## 18. Buteyko Breathing

### Purpose
Improves oxygen delivery to tissues and reduces hyperventilation. Helps with asthma, anxiety, and sleep disorders by normalizing breathing patterns.

### When to Use
- For asthma management
- To reduce hyperventilation
- For better sleep
- During panic attacks
- To improve oxygen efficiency

### Audio-Ready Guided Script (90 seconds)

**[Calm, clinical tones begin]**

"Welcome to Buteyko Breathing. Optimize your breath.

Sit comfortably with your spine tall. Keep your mouth closed throughout.

This technique focuses on gentle, reduced breathing to improve oxygen delivery.

Take a small, gentle breath in through your nose...

Now a small, gentle breath out...

Now hold your breath after the exhale. Pinch your nose if needed.

Hold until you feel a gentle urge to breathe... not until you're desperate.

Now release and breathe normally for ten seconds.

Again. Small breath in... small breath out...

Hold your breath... wait for the gentle urge...

Release and breathe normally.

Once more. Small breath in... small breath out...

Hold... feel the gentle air hunger...

Release and breathe normally.

Notice how your breathing has become lighter, more efficient.

Your body is learning to use oxygen more effectively.

**[Tones fade out]**"

### Visual Animation Cues
- **Central Element**: A subtle nose and airway visualization
- **Gentle Breathing**: Minimal, controlled airflow shown
- **Breath Hold**: Static image with subtle pulse, timer counting up
- **Air Hunger Indicator**: Gentle yellow warning when urge to breathe approaches
- **Recovery Phase**: Normal breathing pattern resumes
- **Background**: Clean medical blue with subtle oxygen molecule patterns
- **Text Overlay**: "Small breath... Hold... Release"
- **Audio Sync**: Minimal breath sounds, gentle pulse during hold
