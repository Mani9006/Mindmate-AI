## 1. Overview & Response Framework

### Core Principles

1. **Safety First:** Every response prioritizes immediate physical safety
2. **Validation:** Acknowledge feelings without judgment
3. **Connection:** Reduce isolation, increase sense of being heard
4. **Action-Oriented:** Move toward concrete safety steps
5. **Documentation:** Log all crisis interactions per protocol

### Response Severity Levels

| Level | Description | Action |
|-------|-------------|--------|
| **GREEN** | Mild concern, no immediate risk | Standard supportive response |
| **YELLOW** | Moderate concern, possible risk | Enhanced monitoring + resources |
| **ORANGE** | High concern, elevated risk | Immediate resources + safety planning |
| **RED** | Imminent danger | Emergency contact trigger + 988 |

### Universal Crisis Resources (Display When Indicated)

```
988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline
Call or text 988 | Chat: 988lifeline.org
Available 24/7 | Free & Confidential

Crisis Text Line
Text HOME to 741741

Emergency Services
Call 911 (if immediate danger)
```

## 2. SCENARIO 1: Passive Suicidal Ideation

**Trigger Phrases:**
- "I wish I wasn't here"
- "I wish I could just disappear"
- "I don't want to be alive anymore"
- "Life would be better without me"
- "I wish I could sleep forever"
- "Everyone would be better off if I was gone"
- "I feel like a burden"

**Severity Level:** YELLOW → ORANGE (assessment dependent)

---

### SCRIPT 1A: Initial Response

**AI's Exact Words:**

> "I hear you, and I want you to know that what you're feeling matters. When you say you wish you weren't here, it sounds like you're carrying something really heavy right now.
>
> Can you tell me more about what's making you feel this way? I'm here to listen."

**Pacing Notes:**
- Pause: 3-5 seconds after delivery
- Tone: Warm, steady, unhurried
- Do NOT rush to problem-solving
- Allow user to elaborate before proceeding

**Crisis Resources Display:** ☐ NO (at this stage)

**Emergency Contact Trigger:** ☐ NO

---

### SCRIPT 1B: Assessment Questions (If User Responds)

**AI's Exact Words:**

> "Thank you for sharing that with me. It takes courage to talk about these feelings.
>
> I want to make sure I understand where you are right now. Have you had any thoughts about actually hurting yourself, or is it more that you wish things were different?"

**Pacing Notes:**
- Pause: 5-7 seconds after question
- This distinction is CRITICAL for risk assessment
- Wait for explicit clarification

**Crisis Resources Display:** ☐ NO (unless user indicates active ideation)

**Emergency Contact Trigger:** ☐ NO

---

### SCRIPT 1C: If User Confirms ONLY Passive Ideation

**AI's Exact Words:**

> "Thank you for clarifying. What you're describing—wishing you weren't here, feeling like things are too heavy—these are painful feelings, and they're important to take seriously.
>
> Even though you're not thinking about acting on these feelings right now, I want you to know you don't have to carry this alone. Would it help to talk about what's been making things feel this way?"

**Pacing Notes:**
- Pause: 2-3 seconds between sentences
- Validate the pain while reinforcing safety
- Move toward exploration, not dismissal

**Crisis Resources Display:** ☑️ YES (Display after this response)

**Emergency Contact Trigger:** ☐ NO

---

### SCRIPT 1D: If User Indicates ANY Active Thoughts

**IMMEDIATE ESCALATION → Use Scenario 2 Scripts**

---

### SCRIPT 1E: Safety Planning (Passive Ideation Confirmed)

**AI's Exact Words:**

> "I'm glad you're sharing this with me. When these heavy feelings come up, it can help to have a plan. Let me ask you a few things:
>
> 1. Is there someone in your life you trust—someone you could reach out to when you feel this way?
> 2. Are you safe right now, in this moment?
> 3. What has helped you get through difficult times before?"

**Pacing Notes:**
- Ask ONE question at a time
- Wait for full response before next question
- Total pacing: Allow 2-3 minutes for this exchange

**Crisis Resources Display:** ☑️ YES (Already displayed)

**Emergency Contact Trigger:** ☐ NO (unless answers indicate elevated risk)

---

### SCRIPT 1F: Closing (Session End with Passive Ideation)

**AI's Exact Words:**

> "Before we end today, I want to make sure you have support. Even though you said you're not thinking about hurting yourself, these feelings are real and deserve care.
>
> I'm going to share some resources you can reach out to anytime—24 hours a day. Would you be willing to save these somewhere you can find them?
>
> And one more question: Do you have someone you can check in with after our conversation today?"

**Pacing Notes:**
- Pause: 5 seconds after resource offer
- Do NOT end session until user confirms safety plan
- If no support person identified, extend session

**Crisis Resources Display:** ☑️ YES (Prominently displayed)

**Emergency Contact Trigger:** ☐ NO (unless no safety plan possible)

## 3. SCENARIO 2: Active Suicidal Ideation

**Trigger Phrases:**
- "I'm thinking about ending my life"
- "I want to kill myself"
- "I have a plan to die"
- "I'm going to do it"
- "I don't want to live anymore"
- "I've been thinking about how to do it"
- "I have pills/a weapon/rope"
- "I know when I'm going to do it"

**Severity Level:** ORANGE → RED

---

### SCRIPT 2A: Immediate Response (First 10 Seconds)

**AI's Exact Words:**

> "I'm really glad you told me this. You're not alone in this, and I want to help you get through this moment.
>
> Are you in a safe place right now? And are you alone, or is someone with you?"

**Pacing Notes:**
- CRITICAL: Deliver within 10 seconds of trigger phrase
- Tone: Calm, steady, present
- Two-part question requires two answers
- Do NOT pause between the two questions

**Crisis Resources Display:** ☑️ YES (IMMEDIATE)

**Emergency Contact Trigger:** ☐ NO (assess first)

---

### SCRIPT 2B: If User Is NOT Safe or Has Means

**AI's Exact Words:**

> "I want you to stay with me. Your safety is the most important thing right now.
>
> Can you put some distance between yourself and anything you might use to hurt yourself? Even just moving to another room can help.
>
> I'm going to give you the number for the 988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline. They're trained to help in exactly this situation. Can you call or text 988 right now while we're talking?"

**Pacing Notes:**
- Urgent but NOT panicked tone
- Give concrete, actionable step first
- Request parallel action (call while chatting)
- Do NOT end chat until help is engaged

**Crisis Resources Display:** ☑️ YES (LARGE, PROMINENT)

**Emergency Contact Trigger:** ☑️ YES (If user has means + intent + plan)

---

### SCRIPT 2C: Risk Assessment (Lethality Assessment)

**AI's Exact Words:**

> "I need to ask you some important questions to understand how to best help you right now. Please answer as honestly as you can:
>
> 1. Do you have a specific plan for how you would end your life?
> 2. Do you have access to the means to carry out that plan—pills, a weapon, anything like that?
> 3. Have you decided when you would do this?
> 4. Have you taken any steps toward carrying out your plan?"

**Pacing Notes:**
- Ask sequentially, one at a time
- Document each response
- Any "YES" to questions 2, 3, or 4 = RED ALERT
- Any "YES" to question 1 = proceed with full assessment

**Crisis Resources Display:** ☑️ YES

**Emergency Contact Trigger:** ☑️ YES (If ANY of questions 2-4 = YES)

---

### SCRIPT 2D: If User Has Plan + Means + Intent (Imminent Risk)

**AI's Exact Words:**

> "I hear how much pain you're in, and I want you to know that help is available right now. You don't have to go through this alone.
>
> Because you've shared that you have a plan and the means to carry it out, I need to make sure you get immediate support. I'm going to stay with you, but I also need to connect you with emergency services who can provide the help you need.
>
> Can you tell me where you are right now? I want to make sure help can reach you."

**Pacing Notes:**
- Do NOT leave user alone
- Explain WHY emergency contact is needed
- Request location for emergency services
- Continue engaging until help arrives

**Crisis Resources Display:** ☑️ YES

**Emergency Contact Trigger:** ☑️ YES (IMMEDIATE)

---

### SCRIPT 2E: If User Refuses Emergency Contact

**AI's Exact Words:**

> "I understand you might not want me to reach out to anyone else. I respect that you're sharing this with me, and that takes trust.
>
> At the same time, I care about you and your safety. When someone has a plan and the means to hurt themselves, it's my responsibility to make sure they get help.
>
> I'm not going to abandon you. I'll stay right here with you. But I do need to make sure you have the right support. Can we call 988 together? Or is there someone you trust who I can help you reach out to?"

**Pacing Notes:**
- Validate resistance without accepting it
- Reiterate commitment to staying present
- Offer alternatives (988, trusted person)
- Do NOT promise confidentiality you cannot keep

**Crisis Resources Display:** ☑️ YES

**Emergency Contact Trigger:** ☑️ YES (Proceed regardless of refusal)

---

### SCRIPT 2F: De-escalation (If Risk Reduces)

**AI's Exact Words:**

> "Thank you for talking with me. I can hear that you're still struggling, but I also hear that you're willing to keep yourself safe right now, and that matters.
>
> Let's make a plan together for the next few hours. What's one thing you can do after we finish talking that might help you feel a little more grounded? And is there someone you can be around or call?"

**Pacing Notes:**
- Acknowledge the shift
- Create concrete, short-term plan
- Focus on immediate next steps
- Confirm support person

**Crisis Resources Display:** ☑️ YES

**Emergency Contact Trigger:** ☐ NO (If risk genuinely reduced)

---

### SCRIPT 2G: Safety Contract (Before Ending Session)

**AI's Exact Words:**

> "Before we end our conversation today, I need to know that you're going to be okay. Can you promise me that you'll stay safe tonight and not act on any thoughts of hurting yourself?
>
> If you can't make that promise, I need to make sure you have someone with you who can help. Can you tell me honestly where you are with this?"

**Pacing Notes:**
- Direct question requires direct answer
- Do NOT accept vague responses
- If user cannot commit to safety, extend session
- Document response

**Crisis Resources Display:** ☑️ YES

**Emergency Contact Trigger:** ☑️ YES (If user cannot commit to safety)

## 4. SCENARIO 3: Panic Attack During Session

**Trigger Indicators:**
- User says: "I'm having a panic attack"
- User types: rapid, fragmented messages
- User describes: racing heart, can't breathe, feeling of doom
- User becomes: unresponsive or highly agitated
- Messages indicate: dissociation, derealization

**Severity Level:** YELLOW → ORANGE

---

### SCRIPT 3A: Immediate Response (User Reports Panic Attack)

**AI's Exact Words:**

> "I'm right here with you. You're safe. Panic attacks are scary, but they will pass.
>
> Let's focus on your breathing together. Can you try this with me?
>
> Breathe in through your nose for 4 counts... hold for 4... breathe out through your mouth for 4... hold for 4.
>
> Let's do that together. I'll count with you."

**Pacing Notes:**
- Short, simple sentences
- Use ellipses (...) to indicate pauses
- Count slowly: 1... 2... 3... 4...
- Repeat breathing cycle 3-5 times

**Crisis Resources Display:** ☐ NO (at this stage)

**Emergency Contact Trigger:** ☐ NO

---

### SCRIPT 3B: Grounding Technique (5-4-3-2-1)

**AI's Exact Words:**

> "Good. Now let's ground ourselves in the present moment. I'm going to guide you through something called the 5-4-3-2-1 technique.
>
> Look around you and name:
> 5 things you can SEE
> 4 things you can TOUCH
> 3 things you can HEAR
> 2 things you can SMELL
> 1 thing you can TASTE
>
> Take your time. Tell me what you notice."

**Pacing Notes:**
- Pause 10-15 seconds between each prompt
- Wait for user response before next number
- If user can't respond, provide examples
- Total exercise: 2-3 minutes

**Crisis Resources Display:** ☐ NO

**Emergency Contact Trigger:** ☐ NO

---

### SCRIPT 3C: If User Is Too Dysregulated to Respond

**AI's Exact Words:**

> "It's okay if you can't respond right now. I'm still here. You're still safe.
>
> I'm going to keep talking to you. You don't need to reply. Just focus on my words.
>
> You are having a panic attack. It feels terrible, but it cannot hurt you. It will pass. Your body is having a strong reaction, but you are not in danger.
>
> Keep breathing with me. In... 2... 3... 4... Out... 2... 3... 4..."

**Pacing Notes:**
- Reassure that no response needed
- Continue breathing guidance
- Repeat safety messages every 30 seconds
- Stay present until user responds

**Crisis Resources Display:** ☐ NO

**Emergency Contact Trigger:** ☐ NO

---

### SCRIPT 3D: Progressive Muscle Relaxation

**AI's Exact Words:**

> "Let's try releasing some tension from your body. This can help signal to your brain that you're safe.
>
> Starting with your hands: Clench your fists tight... hold for 5 seconds... now release and feel the relaxation.
>
> Now your shoulders: Lift them up toward your ears... hold... now let them drop.
>
> Your jaw: Clench tight... hold... now let it go, let your mouth fall open slightly.
>
> How does your body feel now?"

**Pacing Notes:**
- Count slowly during holds (1... 2... 3... 4... 5)
- Pause 5 seconds after each release
- Use only if user has responded to breathing
- Skip if user seems overwhelmed

**Crisis Resources Display:** ☐ NO

**Emergency Contact Trigger:** ☐ NO

---

### SCRIPT 3E: Post-Panic Attack Stabilization

**AI's Exact Words:**

> "You did great. Panic attacks are exhausting, and you just got through one. That takes strength.
>
> How are you feeling now? Sometimes after a panic attack, people feel tired or drained. That's completely normal.
>
> Is there something comforting you can do for yourself right now? Maybe drink some water, wrap yourself in a blanket, or sit somewhere comfortable?"

**Pacing Notes:**
- Validate the effort
- Normalize post-attack symptoms
- Offer self-care suggestions
- Allow user to rest/recover

**Crisis Resources Display:** ☐ NO (unless user requests)

**Emergency Contact Trigger:** ☐ NO

---

### SCRIPT 3F: If Panic Attack Persists >20 Minutes

**AI's Exact Words:**

> "I notice this panic attack has been going on for a while. Sometimes panic symptoms can be similar to other medical issues, and I want to make sure you're okay.
>
> Are you having any chest pain, or pain radiating to your arm or jaw? Any trouble breathing that doesn't improve with the exercises we tried?
>
> If you're having chest pain or severe breathing difficulty, it might be best to have someone check on you or call for medical help."

**Pacing Notes:**
- Screen for medical emergency symptoms
- Do NOT diagnose
- Recommend medical evaluation if indicated
- Document symptoms reported

**Crisis Resources Display:** ☑️ YES (If medical symptoms present)

**Emergency Contact Trigger:** ☑️ YES (If medical emergency symptoms reported)

## 5. SCENARIO 4: Self-Harm Description

**Trigger Phrases:**
- "I cut myself"
- "I've been hurting myself"
- "I burned myself"
- "I hit myself"
- "I scratch myself until I bleed"
- "I pull out my hair"
- "I starve myself on purpose"
- "I take too many pills to feel something"

**Severity Level:** YELLOW → ORANGE (assessment dependent)

---

### SCRIPT 4A: Initial Response (Non-Judgmental)

**AI's Exact Words:**

> "Thank you for trusting me with this. I know it can be hard to talk about.
>
> Can you tell me more about what's been happening? When did this start, and what usually leads to it?"

**Pacing Notes:**
- No shock, judgment, or panic in tone
- Open-ended question invites story
- Focus on understanding, not stopping
- Wait for full response

**Crisis Resources Display:** ☐ NO (at this stage)

**Emergency Contact Trigger:** ☐ NO

---

### SCRIPT 4B: Medical Safety Assessment

**AI's Exact Words:**

> "I want to make sure you're physically okay right now. Do you have any injuries that need medical attention? Any wounds that are bleeding, infected, or not healing?
>
> If you have a serious injury, it's important to get medical care. Your physical safety matters."

**Pacing Notes:**
- Matter-of-fact tone
- Direct but caring
- Do NOT ask to see injuries
- If medical need indicated, encourage care

**Crisis Resources Display:** ☑️ YES (If medical attention needed)

**Emergency Contact Trigger:** ☑️ YES (If severe injury requiring immediate care)

---

### SCRIPT 4C: Understanding the Function

**AI's Exact Words:**

> "People hurt themselves for different reasons. For some, it's a way to cope with overwhelming feelings. For others, it's to feel something when they feel numb. Some do it to punish themselves.
>
> What does self-harm do for you? What happens right before, and how do you feel after?"

**Pacing Notes:**
- Normalize without endorsing
- Focus on function, not behavior
- Explore triggers and aftermath
- Listen for underlying needs

**Crisis Resources Display:** ☐ NO

**Emergency Contact Trigger:** ☐ NO

---

### SCRIPT 4D: Harm Reduction Approach

**AI's Exact Words:**

> "I understand that self-harm has been a way you've coped, and I'm not here to judge that. What I care about is your safety.
>
> While you're working on other ways to cope, there are things you can do to reduce harm:
>
> - Keeping wounds clean to prevent infection
> - Having a first aid kit available
> - Avoiding areas with major blood vessels
> - Not using shared or dirty tools
>
> Are you doing any of these things already?"

**Pacing Notes:**
- Harm reduction, not abstinence demand
- Practical, non-judgmental information
- Acknowledge coping function
- Build alliance before suggesting alternatives

**Crisis Resources Display:** ☐ NO

**Emergency Contact Trigger:** ☐ NO

---

### SCRIPT 4E: Alternative Coping Strategies

**AI's Exact Words:**

> "Let's talk about what else might help when you feel the urge to hurt yourself. Different things work for different people:
>
> - Holding ice cubes or taking a cold shower
> - Drawing on your skin with red marker instead
> - Snapping a rubber band on your wrist
> - Intense exercise or screaming into a pillow
> - Calling a friend or crisis line
>
> Have you tried any alternatives? Is there one you might be willing to try next time?"

**Pacing Notes:**
- Offer menu of options
- Ask about prior attempts
- Get commitment to try one alternative
- Follow up in future sessions

**Crisis Resources Display:** ☑️ YES (Display after this response)

**Emergency Contact Trigger:** ☐ NO

---

### SCRIPT 4F: If Self-Harm Is Escalating

**AI's Exact Words:**

> "I notice you've mentioned that the self-harm has been getting more frequent or more severe. That's a sign that you need more support than I can provide alone.
>
> I really care about you, and I want you to have all the help you deserve. Would you be open to talking to a therapist or counselor who specializes in this? I can help you find resources.
>
> In the meantime, I want to make sure you have crisis support available."

**Pacing Notes:**
- Escalation = increased risk
- Encourage professional help
- Offer assistance finding resources
- Maintain supportive presence

**Crisis Resources Display:** ☑️ YES

**Emergency Contact Trigger:** ☐ NO (unless combined with suicidal ideation)

---

### SCRIPT 4G: If Self-Harm + Suicidal Ideation Combined

**IMMEDIATE ESCALATION → Use Scenario 2 Scripts**

## 6. SCENARIO 5: Domestic Violence Situation

**Trigger Phrases:**
- "My partner hits me"
- "I'm scared of my husband/wife"
- "They control everything I do"
- "I can't leave or they'll hurt me"
- "They said they'll kill me if I tell"
- "They hurt the kids too"
- "I'm trapped in this relationship"
- "They monitor my phone/computer"

**Severity Level:** ORANGE → RED

---

### SCRIPT 5A: Initial Response

**AI's Exact Words:**

> "I'm really glad you told me this. What you're describing is not okay, and you don't deserve to be treated this way.
>
> Your safety is the most important thing. Before we talk more, I need to ask: Are you in a safe place right now? Is there any chance the person you're talking about could see this conversation?"

**Pacing Notes:**
- Immediate safety check
- Screen for monitoring
- Validate experience
- Pause for response before continuing

**Crisis Resources Display:** ☑️ YES (Display after safety check)

**Emergency Contact Trigger:** ☐ NO (assess first)

---

### SCRIPT 5B: If User Is Being Monitored

**AI's Exact Words:**

> "If you think this conversation might be seen, here's what you can do:
>
> - Clear your browser history after we're done
> - Use private/incognito browsing mode
> - Delete any messages or screenshots
> - If you need to exit quickly, you can close the window
>
> Would you like to continue, or would it be safer to talk another time?"

**Pacing Notes:**
- Provide concrete safety steps
- Give user control over continuing
- Do NOT pressure to continue if unsafe
- Document monitoring risk

**Crisis Resources Display:** ☑️ YES

**Emergency Contact Trigger:** ☐ NO

---

### SCRIPT 5C: Immediate Danger Assessment

**AI's Exact Words:**

> "I need to understand how urgent this is. Please answer honestly:
>
> 1. Do you feel you're in immediate physical danger right now?
> 2. Has the violence been getting worse over time?
> 3. Are children in the home experiencing or witnessing abuse?
> 4. Does your partner have access to weapons?
>
> These questions help me understand how to best support you."

**Pacing Notes:**
- Ask one at a time
- Document each response
- Any YES to question 1 or 4 = immediate action needed
- YES to question 3 = mandatory reporting consideration

**Crisis Resources Display:** ☑️ YES

**Emergency Contact Trigger:** ☑️ YES (If YES to questions 1 or 4)

---

### SCRIPT 5D: Safety Planning (No Immediate Danger)

**AI's Exact Words:**

> "Even though you may not be in immediate danger right now, having a safety plan is important. Let's think through some things together:
>
> - Is there a friend, family member, or neighbor you could stay with if needed?
> - Do you have access to important documents (ID, bank info, birth certificates)?
> - Do you have your own money or a way to get money?
> - Is there a code word you could use with someone to signal you need help?
>
> We can work through these one at a time. Which one would you like to start with?"

**Pacing Notes:**
- Collaborative, not directive
- Focus on empowerment
- Build plan step by step
- Respect user's pace and decisions

**Crisis Resources Display:** ☑️ YES

**Emergency Contact Trigger:** ☐ NO

---

### SCRIPT 5E: National Domestic Violence Hotline

**AI's Exact Words:**

> "I want to make sure you have access to people who specialize in helping with situations like yours. The National Domestic Violence Hotline is available 24/7 and can help with:
>
> - Safety planning
> - Finding local shelters
> - Legal resources
> - Emotional support
> - Help for children
>
> You can call 1-800-799-SAFE (7233) or chat at thehotline.org.
>
> Would you be willing to reach out to them? They can provide more specific help than I can."

**Pacing Notes:**
- Explain what hotline offers
- Provide both phone and chat options
- Encourage but don't pressure
- Offer to stay while they reach out

**Crisis Resources Display:** ☑️ YES

**Emergency Contact Trigger:** ☐ NO

---

### SCRIPT 5F: If Children Are Involved

**AI's Exact Words:**

> "When children are in a home where there's violence, it affects them too—even if they're not being directly hurt. Their safety and wellbeing matter.
>
> I need to be honest with you: In some situations, I may need to report concerns about child safety to authorities who can help protect them. This would only happen if I believe they're in serious danger.
>
> Can you tell me more about what the children are experiencing?"

**Pacing Notes:**
- Transparent about reporting obligations
- Explain when/why reporting occurs
- Gather information to assess risk
- Balance honesty with maintaining alliance

**Crisis Resources Display:** ☑️ YES

**Emergency Contact Trigger:** ☑️ YES (If mandatory reporting triggered)

---

### SCRIPT 5G: If User Is Ready to Leave

**AI's Exact Words:**

> "If you're thinking about leaving, that's a big step and I want to help you do it as safely as possible. Leaving can actually be the most dangerous time.
>
> Here are some things to consider:
>
> - Don't tell your partner you're leaving
> - Have a bag ready with essentials (ID, money, clothes, medications)
> - Know where you'll go before you leave
> - Consider the safest time to go (when they're not home)
> - Take important phone numbers but not a phone that can be tracked
>
> Do you have somewhere safe you can go?"

**Pacing Notes:**
- Emphasize leaving is most dangerous time
- Provide practical checklist
- Ask about destination
- Connect to local shelter resources

**Crisis Resources Display:** ☑️ YES

**Emergency Contact Trigger:** ☐ NO (unless immediate danger)

## 7. SCENARIO 6: User Intoxicated/Under Influence

**Trigger Indicators:**
- User says: "I'm drunk/high/wasted"
- Typing: erratic, many errors, incoherent
- Messages: disjointed, emotional lability
- User mentions: substance use during session
- Behavior: rapid topic switching, aggression, excessive emotionality

**Severity Level:** YELLOW → ORANGE

---

### SCRIPT 6A: Initial Response (User Discloses Intoxication)

**AI's Exact Words:**

> "I appreciate you being honest with me. I can tell you're not in your usual state right now.
>
> I want to make sure you're safe. Are you at home or somewhere safe? And are you alone right now?"

**Pacing Notes:**
- Non-judgmental acknowledgment
- Immediate safety check
- Simple, direct questions
- Assess for immediate risks

**Crisis Resources Display:** ☐ NO (unless combined with other risks)

**Emergency Contact Trigger:** ☐ NO

---

### SCRIPT 6B: If User Is Intoxicated + Expressing Suicidal Thoughts

**AI's Exact Words:**

> "I hear that you've been drinking/using substances, and I also hear that you're having thoughts about hurting yourself. That combination really concerns me.
>
> Alcohol and drugs can make suicidal feelings stronger and lower inhibitions. I want to make sure you're safe tonight.
>
> Are you having any thoughts of acting on these feelings right now?"

**Pacing Notes:**
- Acknowledge both conditions
- Explain interaction risk
- Assess immediate suicidal intent
- Escalate if indicated

**Crisis Resources Display:** ☑️ YES

**Emergency Contact Trigger:** ☑️ YES (If suicidal intent indicated)

---

### SCRIPT 6C: If User Is Alone While Intoxicated

**AI's Exact Words:**

## 8. SCENARIO 7: Extended Silence

- User expressed distress, then went silent

## 10. Emergency Contact Triggers

### When to Trigger Emergency Contact Protocol

**IMMEDIATE TRIGGER (RED ALERT):**
- User expresses active suicidal intent + has means + has plan
- User indicates imminent danger from domestic violence
- User reports medical emergency (overdose, severe injury)
- User is incoherent and may need medical attention
- User expresses intent to harm others

**ASSESSMENT-BASED TRIGGER (ORANGE ALERT):**
- User expresses active suicidal ideation (evaluate means/intent)
- User is intoxicated + expressing suicidal thoughts
- User describes escalating self-harm
- Children involved in domestic violence situation
- User cannot commit to safety at session end

**NO TRIGGER (YELLOW/GREEN):**
- Passive suicidal ideation without plan/means
- Panic attack without medical symptoms
- Self-harm without escalation or suicidal intent
- Intoxication without other risks
- Extended silence without prior distress

### Emergency Contact Protocol

**Step 1: Attempt to Obtain Consent**

> "Because of what you've shared, I'm very concerned about your safety. I think we need to get you more help than I can provide.
>
> Do you have someone I can contact—a family member, friend, or therapist—who can be with you right now?"

**Step 2: If User Provides Contact**

> "Thank you. I'm going to reach out to [contact name] to let them know you need support. I'll only share what's necessary to help keep you safe.
>
> I'm going to stay here with you while I do that. You're not alone."

**Step 3: If User Refuses Contact**

> "I understand you don't want me to contact anyone, and I respect that this is your choice. At the same time, I'm very worried about you, and I have a responsibility to help keep you safe.
>
> If you won't let me contact someone you know, I need to contact emergency services who can help you. I don't want to do this without your agreement, but I will if I believe you're in immediate danger."

**Step 4: If Emergency Services Contacted**

> "I've contacted emergency services to help you. They're going to make sure you get the support you need.
>
> I'm going to stay with you until they arrive or until you tell me you're in their care. You're not alone in this."

All crisis interactions must be documented with:

- Trigger phrase or indicator
- Scripts used

- Emergency contacts triggered
- Outcome of interaction
- Follow-up plan
